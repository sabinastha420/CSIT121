What is it important?

  • Enhanced Security: The model uses cryptographic methods to ensure the authenticity of credentials, reducing risks like forgery.
  • Privacy Protection: It allows individuals to share only the necessary parts of their information, preserving data privacy. For example, proving age without revealing a full date of birth.
  • Interoperability: It works across platforms and industries, supporting global adoption.
  • Decentralization: Credentials can be stored directly by individuals, removing the need for a central authority.
